Introduction

Purpose

      This project deals with the development of an inventory management system for a single, mid-sized, supermarket. The system currently in use is paper based and tedious to track and account for goods. The supermarket wishes to automate the task by implementing a desktop application to aid the workers in their job and eliminate the paper-based system. The supermarket has many supplies which it needs to track; examples of such items are:

  1. Dry Goods
  2. Meats
  3. Vegetables
  4. Cleaning Products

      The supermarket warehouse department has a varied number of staff each with designated roles. The roles which are present in the warehouse are:

  1. Warehouse Manager
  2. Product Clerk
  3. Waerhouse Attendant

      The warehouse receives goods from various suppliers and it is the job of the Product Clerk to place the orders for certain stock items. The clerks would receive the items once they are delivered and enter them into the inventory system. The warehouse attendants are responsible for guiding the suppliers to the storage location of their product if or when they need to return said product. The warehouse manager keeps track of all purchases, deliveries and returns.

Documentation Conventions

      The documentation conventions which will be used in this project are Unified Modelling Language (UML) for diagrams, and American Psychological Association (APA) format. UML is a language used among business analysts, software architects and developers. It is used to specify, design and document existing or new business processes, structure and behavior or artifacts of software systems. APA formatting is the style mainly used in within the social sciences for citing sources.
